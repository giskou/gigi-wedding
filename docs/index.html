Our Story

Giota and Giannis met under some quite unexpected circumstances in Gothenburg, Sweden, back in 2015.

He was living in Oslo, Norway. She was living in Freiburg, Germany. Even though Giota was not very impressed initially, Giannis' dedication made her reconsider. Their first year was mostly in remote, with Giannis visiting quite often. As things started to get more serious, Giannis convinced Giota to move to Oslo, which happened in late 2016. In December 2020, right before the pandemic was a thing, Giannis asked the question and Giota said yes!

Fast forward some global lockdowns, pregnancies and civil weddings, we are now happy to invite you to the official "Greek" wedding.

✈ Airports

There are 4 airports that can be used to get as close as possible to Volos.

ATH Athens

Connections with Car, Bus and Train

SKG Thessaloniki

Connections with Car, Bus and Train

JSI Skiathos

Connections with Boat. Also check Restplasser in Apollo.no

VOL Volos/N. Anchialos

Connections with local Bus which will get you to Volos in ~20min

🚗 Car

From Thessaloniki, you drive south the main highway (A1) Until you find the exit for Volos/Velestino (E92). There are quite a few car rental places around the airport that are not included in any aggregated search website. Just spend some time finding their main webpages from their entries in the map. They are quite cheaper than the standard Hertz/Avis etc.

Similarly from Athens, you drive north the main highway (A1) Until you find the exit for N.Anchialos (30).

🚂 Train

There is a connection to Larisa with only one line that runs 7 times a day, starting from 07:12, with the last scheduled train leaving at 23:22.

Unfortunately there is no train routes between Larisa and Volos. The service has not recovered since the (not so) recent flooding. There is bus for train offered by the train company but standard buses are better and recommended.

Coming from Thessaloniki or Athens, you can get the train that connects those two cities and change at Larisa.

Tickets can be purchased from here.

In general, the public trust on the trains in Greece has been shaken a bit due to a horrible accident that happened last year. I would recommend a different way of transport.
